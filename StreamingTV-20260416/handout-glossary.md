# Glossary

- **[Digital Video Recorder (DVR)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_video_recorder)**:

  Records video to permanent storage.  May be a dedicated unit or
  software running on a computer.

- **[Electronic Programming Guide (EPG)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_program_guide)**:

  A menu-based display that allows the user to see current and
  upcoming video programs.  The display may be non-interactive only
  displaying content.  Or may be interactive (sometimes referred
  to an IPG) allowing the user to select programs for viewing or
  potentially recording.

- **[Internet Protocol television (IPTV)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_Protocol_television)**(1) (industry):

  A streaming television service provided over a managed, private
  network.  The service and the network are owned by the same entity.

  Examples: Fios TV, AT&T U-verse
  
- **[Internet Protocol television (IPTV)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_Protocol_television#Definition)**(2) (common):

  A streaming television service over the public Internet.

  This definition is often used by the public when meaning an
  **over-the-top media (OTT)** service.

- **IPTV box**:

  A [set-top box](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M3U)
  for displaying **IPTV** content.

  Examples: Smart TV, Apple TV, Amazon Firestick, NVIDIA Shield,
  or Google Chromecast.

- **[Over-the-top media service (OTT)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Over-the-top_media_service)**:

  A digital distribution service of video and audio delivered directly
  to viewers via the public Internet.

  FCC classifies **OTT** services into two groups **MVPD** and
  **OVD**.

    - **[Multichannel Video Programming Distributors (MVPD)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multichannel_television_in_the_United_States)**:
 
      Traditional television service providers that use cable or
      satellite dish to deliver their content to their customers.
      These providers are subject to FCC regulations.

    - **[Online Video Distributors (OVD)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_video_distributor)**:

      These are the television service providers that use a network
      to distribute content to their customers.  These are not
      subject to FCC regulations.  Providers primarily fall into
      **AVOD**, **FAST**, or **SVOD**, but may blur their service
      lines by doing more than one type.

        - **[Advertising-based Video On Demand (AVOD)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_on_demand#Advertising_video_on_demand)**:

          These **OVD** providers insert ads for their on-demand
          video content and sometimes require a reduced monthly
          subscription fee.

          Examples: Tubi, Pluto TV, Amazon Freevee, YouTube

        - **[Free ad-supported streaming Television (FAST)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_ad-supported_streaming_television)**:

          These **OVD** providers of streaming video content do not
          require a monthly subscription fee, but instead insert
          ads.

          Examples: Tubi, Pluto TV

        - **[Subscription Video On Demand (SVOD)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_on_demand#Subscription_models)**:

          These **OVD** providers require a monthly subscription and
          often don't have ads, or limited ads for the on-demand
          video content.

          Examples: Netflix, Hulu, Paramount+
